Q11
Describe the rubber ring (elastrator) castration method.

Answer:

Used for: Calves or lambs from birth to 3 weeks of age

Technique:

  • Constrictive rubber ring applied to scrotal neck using elastrator
  • Both testes must be distal to the ring
  • Blood flow is obstructed
  • Testes and scrotum atrophy and drop off after 4-6 weeks

Advantages: Quick and easy

Disadvantages:

  • Slow wound healing
  • Infection risk
  • Band may break
  • Painful in older calves without anesthesia (inhumane)
Q12
Describe the Burdizzo castration method.

Answer:

Technique: Scrotum is NOT opened; testicular vasculature is only crushed, not cut

Suggested for: Immature males 1-3 months

Procedure:

  • Instrument placed across each spermatic cord individually
  • Cord held against lateral wall of scrotum
  • Each cord crushed TWICE midway between testicle and scrotum-belly junction
  • Second crushing 1 cm below first site
  • Burdizzo remains on cord for few seconds

Result: Testes atrophy, scrotum remains intact

Advantages: Eliminates infection and excessive hemorrhage risk when done properly

Disadvantages: Possible injuries/infection, technique failure

Why is the pampiniform plexus important?

Answer:

The pampiniform plexus wraps around the testicular artery and provides countercurrent heat exchange mechanism to assist testicular thermoregulation. This keeps the testes cooler than body temperature, which is essential for sperm production.
