Question 4
Describe the epididymis: its structure, location, and attachments.

Structure:

  • The epididymis is a tightly coiled tube
  • It connects the efferent ducts from the testicle to the vas deferens (ductus deferens)

Location:

  • Adherent to the dorsolateral surface of the testis
  • Head is positioned cranially

  • The ligament of the tail of the epididymis attaches the testis and epididymis to the vaginal tunic and spermatic fascia

Question 14
Describe the anesthetic protocols for castration in male ruminants for all three methods: rubber rings, Burdizzo, and surgical castration.

1. For Rubber Ring Castration:

  • Local anesthetic should be applied before rubber rings are used
  • Purpose: to allow rapid transport of the drug to the spermatic vein and lymphatic

2. For Burdizzo Castration:

  • Local anesthetic injected subcutaneously into the spermatic cord

3. For Surgical Castration:

  • Use of local anesthetic drugs with or without the help of 2% xylazine (IM)
  • Subcutaneous infiltration of the scrotum to block the sensation of scrotal incision line
  • Blockage of nerve fibers in the spermatic cord can be achieved by injection of Lidocaine 2% into each spermatic cord
Question 15
Describe the anesthetic protocol for equine castration, including drug names and doses.

Primary Protocol:

  • Use general anesthesia such as ultra-short acting barbiturate
  • Thiopentone sodium injected IV in the jugular vein
  • Plus local anesthesia of incisional site of the scrotum

Alternative Protocol:

  • Mixture of Ketamine + Xylazine
  • Ketamine: 2.2 mg/kg body weight
  • Xylazine: 1.1 mg/kg body weight
Question 16
Describe the anesthetic protocol for canine castration, including drug names and doses.

Protocol:

  • General anesthesia injectable of mixture of Ketamine-Xylazine is the most common for surgical castration
  • Ketamine dose in dogs: 15 mg/kg body weight
  • Xylazine dose in dogs: 5 mg/kg body weight

Question 18
Describe the rubber ring (elastrator) method: age range, technique, time to effect, advantages, and disadvantages.

Age Range:

  • A bloodless method used for calves or lambs from birth to three weeks of age

Technique:

  • A constrictive rubber ring is applied to the neck of scrotum using special instrument (an elastrator)
  • Both testes must be distal to the ring
  • Blood flow through the testes and scrotum is obstructed by application of pressure around the scrotal neck

Time to Effect:

  • The testes and the scrotum atrophy and drop off after about 4-6 weeks

Advantages:

  • This method is quick and easy to perform

Disadvantages:

  • Slow wound healing
  • Infection risk
  • Band may break
  • Band may not disrupt all circulation to the testicles
  • Pain when banding older calves, especially when used without anesthesia (inhuman and unethical)
Question 19
Describe the Burdizzo method: age range, technique, advantages, disadvantages, and why it's suggested only for immature males.

Key Principle:

  • The scrotum is NOT opened
  • The testicular vasculature is only crushed, not cut

Age Range:

  • Suggested for use only in immature males from 1-3 months
  • Why? Older males have large cremaster muscles that may prevent adequate vasculature crushing

Technique:

  • The instrument should be placed across each spermatic cord individually
  • The cord is held against the lateral wall of the scrotum
  • Each cord is crushed twice about midway between the testicle and the scrotum-belly junction
  • The Burdizzo remains on the cord for a few seconds
  • The second crushing is about 1 cm below the first site to minimize acute pain from a second crush
  • The same technique is repeated on another cord

Result:

  • Testes atrophy and scrotum remains intact

Advantages:

  • When performed properly, eliminates the chance of infection and excessive hemorrhage

Disadvantages:

  • Injuries / infection (although it is a bloodless method)
  • Failure of the technique (male remains intact)

Question 24
Describe the complete step-by-step technique of open surgical castration in ruminants (8 steps).
  1. Step 1: One testis is held firmly to the outside so that the scrotum is pulled tight over the testis
  2. Step 2: With a scalpel make an incision in the scrotum. In ruminants, the incision is made in the distal third of the scrotum (Ø§Ù„Ø«Ù„Ø« Ø§Ù„Ø¨Ø¹ÙŠØ¯)
  3. Step 3: Make pressure to expose the testis covering by tunica vaginalis out of the scrotum
  4. Step 4: OPEN the tunica vaginalis (this is the key difference from closed castration)
  5. Step 5: Blunt dissection to expose the spermatic cord
  6. Step 6: Transfixing ligation of the spermatic cord is performed with non-absorbable suture material such as silk
  7. Step 7: An emasculator is fixed on the cord in front of ligation to crush and cut the cord (emasculator remains about 1-2 minutes) after cutting the cord then removed
  8. Step 8: The testis is removed and the scrotal wound remains opened without suturing which heals more quickly by secondary intention
  9. Step 9: The same technique is repeated on another testis
